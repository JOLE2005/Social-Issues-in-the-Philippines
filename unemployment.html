UNEMPLOYMENT

Unemployment rates went up during this pandemic. According to corporatefinanceinstitute.com, unemployment refers to employable individuals who are actively seeking a job but unable to find a job. The unemployment rates went down in October 2020 but, people who are looking for more jobs rises.
The Philippines' unemployment rate decreased to 8.7% in October, equivalent to about 3.8 million unemployed Filipinos amid the crisis.
While the number of jobless Filipinos went down, underemployment, or the number of employed people but are looking for more work to meet needs, stood at 14.4% in October.
October underemployment was lower than the rates in April and July at 18.9% and 17.3%, respectively. However, the underemployment rate in October 2020 is still higher than the October 2019 rate of 12.8%.
The labor force participation rate (LFPR) in October 2020 was 58.7% or 43.6 million Filipinos who were either employed or unemployed.
This is the second-lowest LFPR reported in the history of the Philippine labor market following the record low of 55.7% reported in April 2020. In October 2019, the LFPR was at 61.4%, translating to 44.6 million Filipinos.
